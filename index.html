Overview

What is Trade Credit Insurance?

When you sell goods or services on credit terms, you take on the risk that your buyer may not pay. Trade credit insurance protects your accounts receivable — covering losses if a customer fails to pay due to insolvency, protracted default, or political risk in cross-border transactions.

Aon's Trade Credit Solutions team helps businesses design, place, and manage these insurance programmes, acting as broker and advisor between you and the world's leading credit insurers.

Whether you are a domestic-focused business or an international exporter, Aon tailors solutions that fit your risk profile, sector, and growth strategy.

Core risks covered

What Does a Policy Protect Against?

Aon-placed trade credit policies can cover a range of commercial and political risks that threaten your receivables.

Buyer Insolvency

Coverage triggered when a customer enters bankruptcy, receivership, or formal insolvency proceedings and cannot meet its payment obligations.

Protracted Default

Covers situations where a solvent buyer simply fails to pay within an agreed period — no formal insolvency required for a claim to be triggered.

Political Risk

Protection in export transactions where government action, currency inconvertibility, war, or civil unrest prevents the buyer from completing payment.

Contract Frustration

Covers pre-shipment risks where a contract is cancelled or disrupted before goods are dispatched, leaving you exposed for goods manufactured or ordered.
